About HackOR

This spring, enjoy a virtual weekend of programming, workshops, mentors, networking, and other fun activities.

HackOR is a 48 hour virtual hackathon where programmers gather to collaborate on a project—organized and chaired by major schools in Oregon, with the goal of bringing hackathons to more students. We are very beginner friendly and encourage all students interested to join!


What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a project-based coding competition over the span of a few days. During the competition, participants team up in groups of four to create a tech solution, which is then presented to a set of judges.

With workshops, mentors, and collaborative teamwork, Hackathons are a great way to build your skills in tech, design, and ideation.


How do I get started?

As a free virtual event, HackOR is open to any and all students enrolled in school. With access to workshops, events, and mentors, there are two ways to participate in HackOR:

  • Hackathon Participants: experiences the Hackathon scene first hand. Hackathon Participants form teams and collaborate on a project, which is then entered in the competition for categories and prizes.
  • Emerging Attendee: offered to any and all students who identifies as a beginner. Emerging attendees have the option of attending the hackathon as a conference and submitting a project for the "Best Beginner Hack" award.

If resources allow, HackOR plans on accomodating all participants interested. We strongly encourage you to apply. Applications for both open from February 1st to February 28th and are admitted on a rolling basis. Once admitted, we will send you more information on the event.

For more information regarding HackOR attendees and teams, please check out our FAQ.
