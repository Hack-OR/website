March 19 - 21, 2021

Oregon’s Premier All-Student Hackathon

HackOR is a 48 hour hackathon where programmers gather to collaborate on a project—organized and chaired by major schools in Oregon. Partnering with Major League Hacking (MLH) and going virtual amidst the pandemic, HackOR aims to bring the best and brightest students in tech together to enjoy a virtual weekend of hacking, workshops, tech talks, networking and other fun activities.

What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a project-based coding competition over the span of a few days. During the competition, participants team up in groups of four to create a tech solution for social impact, which is then presented to a set of judges. There are also workshops throughout the hackathon to build skills and network.

How do I get started?

As a virtual event, HackOR is open to any students enrolled in school in the United States. There are two ways to participate in HackOR:

  • Hackathon Participants: accessible to all students currently enrolled in school and over the age of 14. With access to workshops, event,s and mentors, Hackathon Participants form teams of 4 and work on a project with, which is then entered for judging during the competition for categories and prizes.
  • Emerging Attendee: offered to younger students down to middle school and for beginners who are interested in learning programming and related skills. Emerging attendees have access to attending workshops, events, and browsing hackathon projects.

Applications for both open from February 1st to February 28th and are admitted on a rolling basis. Once admitted, we will send you more information on the event.
