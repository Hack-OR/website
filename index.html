About HackOR

This spring, we’ll be welcoming students from all over the world, regardless of your experience or backgrounds, to enjoy a virtual weekend of programming, workshops, mentors, networking, and other fun activities.

HackOR is a 48 hour virtual hackathon where programmers of all experience levels and backgrounds gather to collaborate on a project—organized and chaired by major schools in Oregon—with the goal of bringing hackathons to more students. We are very beginner friendly and encourage all students interested to join!

Our theme this year is "Choose Your Adventure, Build a Better World"

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a project-based coding competition over the span of a few days. During the competition, participants team up in groups of four to create a tech solution, which is then presented to a set of judges.

With workshops, mentors, and collaborative teamwork, hackathons are great ways to build your skills in tech, design, and ideation.

How do I get started?

As a free virtual event, HackOR is open to any and all students or recent graduates within 12 months, with access to workshop, events, and mentors.

While Hackors participate in teams of four, you don't need a team to register! HackOR provides a team formation system and social events leading up to the hackathon.

There are many ways to participate in HackOR:

General

Recommended to all students with technical project experience.

Hackors in the general track form teams of four and collaborate on a project, which is then entered in the competition for categories and prizes.

Beginner

Don't have technical experience? That's okay. HackOR offers students a beginner track.

Hackors in the beginner track have the option of attending the hackathon as a conference and submitting a project for the "Best Beginner Hack" award.


Mentor

HackOR is looking for mentors, open to all individuals (professionals/students) with strong technical expertise! Mentors are to be available throughout the weekend and guide students through their projects and questions.

Applications for both open from February 1st to February 28th and are admitted on a rolling basis. We strongly encourage all students to apply!

For more information regarding HackOR attendees and teams, please check out our FAQ.
